Exo Explorer

Built by Lannon Khau

Exo Explorer is a concept-stage platform designed to explore the potential habitability of exoplanets through AI-powered feature engineering and generative imaging. By analyzing NASA's Exoplanet Archive, the app uncovers hidden correlations, builds predictive models, and visually renders what alien worlds might look like—from icy giants to lava planets.

🪐 What Exo Explorer Does

  • Ingest NASA Data: Pulls structured exoplanet data from official archives and reformats for machine learning.
  • Feature Engineering: Constructs new predictive columns like surface temperature, potential water presence, or gravity thresholds.
  • Modeling Habitability: Applies classification models to score potential habitability with transparent logic.
  • AI Planet Rendering: Transforms engineered data into rich text prompts, then sends to image generators to visualize each planet’s unique characteristics.

🧬 Envisioned Tech Stack

  • Flask + FastAPI for backend data processing
  • LangChain & OpenAI for feature logic & narrative prompts
  • Scikit-learn + XGBoost for predictive scoring
  • DALL·E 3 / Stability AI for generative rendering
  • TailwindCSS for sleek and minimal UI
  • MySQL (RDS) for storing planet metadata and user logs

🌌 Who Is This For?

Exo Explorer is for curious minds. Whether you're an astronomy student, a data scientist fascinated by classification, or someone who dreams of alien skies, this app helps you see the invisible patterns hidden in distant star systems.

💫 Why It Matters

We’re discovering new worlds every year—but numbers alone don’t stir the imagination. Exo Explorer bridges the gap between astronomy and storytelling. It brings distant planets into vivid focus, helping users see not just how far we’ve come in space discovery, but how close we are to understanding alien worlds.
